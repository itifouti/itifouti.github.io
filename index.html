I am a postdoctoral researcher in applied mathematics working on adaptive reduced-order modeling for parameterized partial differential equations, with applications to compressible flows and harmonic wave inversion.

I defended my PhD in November 2025 at the Inria center of Université de Bordeaux. My thesis focused on coupling metric-based mesh adaptation with projection-based model reduction for nonlinear hyperbolic problems. It was supervised by Nicolas Barral and Tommaso Taddei.

Research interests

  • Reduced-order modeling
  • Metric-based mesh adaptation
  • Error estimation and adjoint-based methods
  • Simulation of compressible flows
  • Harmonic full-wave inversion
